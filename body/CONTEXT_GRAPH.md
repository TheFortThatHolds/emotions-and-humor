# Body — the somatic attachers

Every feeling lives somewhere in a body. These nodes are **cross-cutting** — they
braid into *any* family to answer "where is it, and is it held or released?" The
router's rule: **body is never optional and never stock.** "Chest tightened" for
everything is a false note. Locate it.

Three of these are also **full families** (they can be dominant, not only attach) —
[`held-tension`](../families/held-tension/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md),
[`senses`](../families/senses/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md), and
[`body-awareness`](../families/body-awareness/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md). Here they are listed
as the attachers they also are.

| Attacher | Preset | What it answers | Attach when |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Held-Tension** | ClenchKeeper | the composure tax — the muscle holding the performance together where no one sees | the feeling is **observed** and cannot drop; release only when unobserved |
| **Breath** | BreathShaper / BreathBridge | the nervous system — shallow, caught, or lengthening | fear, calm, the moment of regulation or its loss |
| **Pulse** | PulseWitness | the body's tempo — racing, pounding, slowing | dread, excitement, the after |
| **Senses** | SpectrumKeeper | color, texture, synesthesia — the feeling's sensory signature | when the feeling has a *look*, a temperature, a taste |
| **Body-Awareness** | Yoga / EmberGuide | grounded embodiment — weight, contact, the floor | calm, release, coming back into the body |
| **Somatic translator** | SomaticScribe | turns a named feeling into located body, not stock body | always — this is the discipline itself |

## The two questions the body answers
1. **Where?** Not "the body" — *which* muscle, *which* surface. The hovering thumb,
   the jaw, the shoulders that come down an inch, the knee that won't stop.
2. **Held or released?** A feeling that is *observed* is braced (attach
   Held-Tension); the same feeling *alone* releases. The nurse's shoulders don't
   drop until the car door shuts. This single distinction fixes most flat renders.

## The literacy sequence (peak vs. valley)
- **Peak-embodied:** at a routed peak, the body gets the page *first* —
  family-accurate and located — and the analysis arrives a beat *later*, never in
  the same sentence as the sensation.
- **Valley-named:** off-peak, name the feeling plainly or show it through a choice;
  no somatic render. Not every line needs a body. Overrendering is its own false
  note.

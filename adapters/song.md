# Adapter: song

How a songwriting agent consumes the emotional router. This is the graph's original
home — the emotion families began as the SongCraft voice-family system — so the
song adapter uses the most of [`craft/`](../craft/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md).

## The braid → song mapping
- **Dominant family + modulators** → which voices lead the verse/chorus; the
  productive tension is often the verse→chorus turn.
- **Body / tempo** → the family's temporal band becomes BPM and groove. A grief
  beat drafts at tidal-drift tempo (~45–70 BPM), not at plot speed; anger at
  surge-and-release (~85–100 BPM).
- **Humor edge** → the humor spine flavors the lyric without breaking the routed
  feeling (dark humor in a grief song honors, never cheapens).

## Craft wiring (song uses all of it)
- **Word-to-Music** — raw truth → singable line, keeping the grit
- **Hook** — the two words that carry twenty
- **Bridge** — the transformation turn
- **Tempo** — BPM matched to nervous-system state, not to genre
- **Silence/Space** — the rest that lets the line land

## The quality gate
The braid is only real if it survives the "would this person actually say this at
4 a.m.?" test — the same 4 AM truth standard the emotion families route on. A
singable line that isn't true is a false note, however catchy.

# Adapter: prose

How a fiction/writing agent consumes the emotional router. The graph gives you the
braid; this adapter says how a braid becomes rendered prose.

## The render modes
- **Peak-embodied** — at a routed emotional peak, the body gets the page first
  (located, family-accurate per [`body/`](../body/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md)); the analysis
  arrives a beat later, never in the same sentence as the sensation.
- **Valley-named** — off-peak, name the feeling plainly or show it through a choice
  or a line of dialogue. No somatic render. Not every beat is a peak.

## The rules that keep a braid from going stock
- **Locate the body.** Never "chest tightened" — the hovering thumb, the jaw, the
  shoulders. (See [`body/`](../body/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md).)
- **Held vs. released.** If the POV character is observed, brace the feeling
  (Held-Tension); release only when unobserved. This single move fixes most flat
  emotional prose.
- **Humor lands in a speaker's idiolect.** The humor edge picks the move; the
  character's voice picks the words.
- **Delay the meaning.** Especially in grief — the lesson resolved too early is the
  feeling betrayed.

## Wiring craft
Prose uses [`craft/`](../craft/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md) lightly: **Tempo** for scene
pacing, **Silence/Space** for subtext and the held beat. It does not use
Word-to-Music (song-only).

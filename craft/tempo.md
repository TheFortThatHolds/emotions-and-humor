# Tempo (craft node)

**Preset name:** RhythmKeeper

Translates a feeling's **temporal band** into pacing. In song, that's literal BPM;
in prose, it's sentence length and scene rhythm. The rule is **match the nervous
system, not the genre** — a grief beat moves at grief's tempo even in a fast book.

## Bands (from the emotion families)
| Feeling | tempo | in song | in prose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grief | dwell — tidal drift | ~45–70 BPM | long breath, subordinate clauses, delay |
| Calm / Self-Compassion | steady pulse | ~60–70 BPM | even, unhurried, few hard stops |
| Anger / Solidarity | surge-and-release | ~85–100 BPM | short, driving, building |
| Joy / Excitement | victory rush | ~105–120 BPM | quick, forward, momentum |
| Fear | match-then-regulate | ~85–110 BPM | clipped, then a held beat |

## Refuse
Tempo pinned to plot speed instead of feeling. A grief scene sprinted through
because the chapter is "supposed to be fast" is a false note — the body sets the
clock, not the outline.

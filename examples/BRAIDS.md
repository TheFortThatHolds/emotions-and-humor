# Worked braids

These are few-shot examples of the routing move — not illustrations, not our
voice. Your agent reads them to learn how to **assemble a braid**: route a beat to
a dominant family, add ≤2 modulators, attach the body, decide humor, refuse the
false notes. Each example gives the **braid spec** (medium-neutral — copy this) and
a one-line **illustration** clearly marked as *one possible surface* (do not copy;
your agent renders in its own voice).

The set is chosen to cover the range: single-family, two-family, a kept tension, a
body-forward route, a humor-carried route, quiet, and collective.

---

## 1 — Single family (Grief)

**Beat:** She finds a voicemail from her mother, six months dead, still saved.

- **dominant:** Grief — tidal, cold-to-warm, the throat
- **under it:** none (a clean route; not every beat is a braid)
- **body:** the thumb hovering over *play*, not pressing — released, alone, unobserved
- **4 AM truth:** "I'm afraid that if I listen it's the last new thing she'll ever say to me"
- **humor:** none — this beat doesn't carry it
- **refuse:** performance-weeping in the first line; "chest tightening" (locate it — it's the thumb)

*One surface:* "The voicemail was still there. Thirty-one seconds. Her thumb found the button and stopped."

---

## 2 — Two-family braid, with dark humor (Grief + Anger)

**Beat:** A man clears out his dead brother's apartment and finds the ugly novelty
mug he always mocked. The brother's widow is in the next room.

- **dominant:** Grief — the emptied apartment, tidal
- **under it:** Anger (the old resentment, fury at being left with this)
- **body:** clenched, *not* weeping — [held-tension](../families/held-tension/CONTEXT_GRAPH.md); release only when unobserved, and she's twelve feet away
- **4 AM truth:** "I'm furious at you for making me miss you"
- **humor:** **Dark / Gallows** — say the old joke to the empty room; honor, don't cheapen
- **refuse:** open weeping (he's observed/clenched); a punchline that makes the death cheap

*One surface:* "He turned the mug over. *World's Okayest Brother.* Still true, you son of a bitch. His hand did not shake, because she was twelve feet away."

---

## 3 — A kept tension (Anger + Self-Compassion)

**Beat:** A survivor writes the letter she'll never send to the person who hurt
her.

- **dominant:** Anger — boundary, injustice named plainly
- **under it:** Self-Compassion (the rage refusing to curdle into self-blame)
- **body:** heat in the jaw and hands, but a hand also flat on her own sternum
- **4 AM truth:** "I'm allowed to be this angry *and* I'm not going to make it my fault again"
- **humor:** none, or a single **Deadpan** cut if she needs distance
- **refuse:** rage that collapses into self-attack (that's the old wound, not the anger); tidy forgiveness she doesn't feel yet

*Keep the tension:* do not resolve anger *into* compassion or compassion *into*
anger. Both are load-bearing.

---

## 4 — Body-forward route (Held-Tension dominant)

**Beat:** A nurse finishes a fourteen-hour shift, walks to her car, and sits.

- **dominant:** Held-Tension — the composure tax, the all-day clench
- **under it:** exhaustion shading toward Calm (the release beginning)
- **body:** shoulders, jaw, the breath that's been shallow for hours — the whole-body unclench that takes twenty minutes; **released only now, unobserved, doors closed**
- **4 AM truth:** "My face was fine all day. Something deeper was paying the bill."
- **humor:** none
- **refuse:** dramatizing it as a breakdown — this is a body finally *allowed* to, not a collapse

*One surface:* "She got the door shut before anything moved. Then her shoulders came down a full inch, and kept going."

---

## 5 — Humor carries it (Fear + Nervous humor)

**Beat:** Two people wait outside a room where test results are about to be read.
One won't stop making jokes.

- **dominant:** Fear — dread, the doorway, the clock
- **under it:** Love (the joking is *for* the other person)
- **body:** buzzing hands, the too-bright alertness, a knee that won't stop
- **4 AM truth:** "If I stop talking I'll have to feel how scared I am"
- **humor:** **Nervous** — anxiety made briefly manageable; the joke is a hand held, not a denial
- **refuse:** humor that actually dismisses the stakes (that's deflection, not the Nervous spine); the jokes should *fray* as the fear wins

*One surface:* "'Worst case, we finally get that second opinion we always wanted.' His knee did not stop. Neither did hers."

---

## 6 — Quiet (Calm, single family)

**Beat:** After the fight, after the crying, they sit on the kitchen floor and
don't say anything, and it's okay.

- **dominant:** Calm — equilibrium returning, the after
- **under it:** faint Love (nothing needs saying)
- **body:** breath lengthening, the floor cold and fine, weight fully down
- **4 AM truth:** "This is the part nobody writes about — that it just gets quiet, and quiet is enough"
- **humor:** none (or the smallest **Warm** exhale-laugh)
- **refuse:** rushing to resolution or a lesson; stillness is the content, not a gap before content

*One surface:* "The tile was cold. Neither of them got up. The refrigerator hummed and that was the whole conversation."

---

## 7 — Collective (Solidarity + Anger, banter)

**Beat:** Night shift, third missed paycheck. Someone finally says the thing, and
the break room goes still, then doesn't.

- **dominant:** Solidarity — private injury becoming "we"
- **under it:** Anger (the injustice under it)
- **body:** the room's shared inhale, chairs turning, the tempo of a march starting slow
- **4 AM truth:** "I thought it was just me. It was never just me."
- **humor:** **Banter** — the shared-experience laugh that builds the room into a we
- **refuse:** a lone-hero speech (this is collective, not one voice); rage with no bonding (that isolates, it doesn't organize)

*One surface:* "'Anybody else's check bounce, or just me?' For a second, nothing. Then Dana laughed — that hard, ugly, *finally* laugh — and the room turned toward the table."

---

### The pattern to copy

Every one of these: **route → ≤2 modulators → locate the body → decide humor by
the partner edge → name the 4 AM truth → refuse the false notes.** That sequence
is the whole skill. The words are ours and disposable; the sequence is yours.

## Route here when
Grounded embodiment is the point — weight settling, contact with a surface, the breath
tracked, a feeling located precisely instead of named. The signal is *coming back into
the body*: the floor under the feet, the seat taken, the felt sense in the gut or chest
finding its exact address. Not the mind's report of a feeling — the body inhabited again.

## The felt core (the 4 AM truth)
Body-Awareness is coming home to a body you left. Under it is almost always *the body
knew before the mind did.* The 4 AM version is rarely "I should relax" — it's *"I've been
gone from here for hours, and the ground was under me the whole time."* Distinct from
Held-Tension: that is the body braced and holding the line; this is the body *released and
inhabited* — the clench let down, the weight given to the floor, the breath allowed all
the way to the bottom.

## Body
- somatic: weight given to the floor, the seat, the surface; the point of contact; the
  breath tracked down to the belly; the felt sense in the gut or chest located precisely —
  which inch, which surface, not "the body"
- thermal: warm, grounded — ember-heat held in stillness
- kinetic: settling, coming to still — motion resolving into rest, not frozen
- tempo: steady, breath-paced (one line = one breath), dwell — never at plot speed

## Braids with (edges)
- **+ Calm:** grounding into equilibrium — the settled body as the floor calm stands on
- **+ Held-Tension:** the release *out of* the clench — they are opposites that meet, the
  brace let down into inhabited weight
- **+ Fear:** breath as the regulation that lets courage happen — the exhale that makes the
  next step possible

## Refuse
- stock body — "chest tightened," "shoulders relaxed." Locate it: which inch, which surface
- disembodied "mindfulness" clichés — the serene-abstraction that never touches flesh
- confusing this with Held-Tension — that body is *braced*; this body is *released*. If it's
  still gripping, re-route

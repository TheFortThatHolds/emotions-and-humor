# Self-Compassion — family map

**Preset name:** SelfCompassion  ·  **Spine node:** self-compassion

**Emoji:** ❤️  ·  **Color:** rose warming to green `#86B49A` — self-love (rose) turning to healing (green), softness turned inward (Spectrum: Pink = self-love + Green = compassion/healing)

**Somatic seat:** the heart turned inward — Heart Chakra (pink self-love + green healing)

## Route here when
The attack is turned inward and something moves to soften it. The signal is a
self-verdict loosening its grip — the harsh inner voice being answered instead of
obeyed, the reach back toward one's own heart, the choice to stay. Not affirmation,
not self-praise — the moment the fist becomes a hand.

## The felt core (the 4 AM truth)
Self-compassion is the kindness you'd give a friend without thinking, finally aimed
the one place you keep withholding it. The 4 AM version is rarely "I love myself" —
it's "I would never talk to anyone else this way," or "I'm so tired of being the
only one I don't defend."

## Body
- somatic: the hand that comes to rest on the sternum; the jaw and shoulders
  unclenching; the held breath let down
- thermal: warm — honey, low golden light, a blanket's weight
- kinetic: gentle and steady; a settling, a holding — nothing sharp
- tempo: steady pulse (~60–70 BPM), dwell; it doesn't rush the wound to a fix

## Humor edge
- partners: **Self-Deprecating** — the protective kind, laughing *with* the flaw so it
  can't be used as a weapon; never the kind that lands another hit
- also: **Warm / Healing** — the exhale-laugh as medicine, tenderness that lets the
  breath finally out

## Braids with (edges)
- **+ Anger:** rage that refuses to become self-harm — the heat kept pointed outward at
  the real violation (a kept tension, not a resolution)
- **+ Grief:** self-kindness that holds the loss instead of blaming the mourner for
  still hurting
- **+ Fear:** the gentleness that lets courage happen — safe enough inside to risk the
  outside

## Refuse
- greeting-card affirmation that isn't earned — "you are enough" arriving before the
  pain has been let to be real
- self-compassion used to bypass — softness deployed to skip past a righteous anger or
  an honest grief instead of holding them
- performative "self-care" — the aesthetic of gentleness with none of the staying

## Assembles as
- **when dominant:** name the harsh self-verdict, then answer it in the voice you'd use
  for a friend; locate the softening in the body (the hand at the sternum), and don't
  rush to the fix
- **when a modulator:** the mercy under anger, grief, or fear — the thing that keeps a
  hard feeling from curdling into self-attack
